[행렬] Idempotent Matrices

Definition
\( A^2 = A \text{where A is a square matrix}\)

Theorem 1

The only non-singular idempotent matrix is I.

proof
\( A=A^{-1}AA=A^{-1}A^2=A^{-1}A=I \)

All other idempotent matrices are singular!

Theorem 2

If A is idempotent, then I-A is idemptoent

proof
\( (I-A)^2 = I-2A+A^2=(I-A) \)

Theorem 3

Let A be square and symmetric.
A is idempotent iff the eigenvalues of A are 0 or 1.

proof
Let \( A = P\Lambda P^T \) be the eigen decompostiion of A. P is orthogonal and \( \Lambda \) is diagonal. \( A^2 = (P\Lambda P^T)^2 = P\Lambda p^TP\Lambda P^T = P\Lambda^2 P^T. \)

\( “\Rightarrow” A^2=P\Lambda^2 P^T=P\Lambda P^T=A \Rightarrow \Lambda^2=\Lambda \Rightarrow \text{diagonal entries are 0 or 1} \\\ “\Leftarrow” \Lambda^2=\Lambda \Rightarrow A=P\Lambda P^T=P\Lambda^2 P^T=A^2 \)

Theorem 4

Let A, B be idempotent matrices. If AB=BA, then AB is idempotent.

proof
\( (AB)^2 = ABAB = A^2B^2 = AB \)

Theorem 5

If A and (-A) are idempotent, the A=[0].

proof
\( A=A^2=(-1)^2A^2=(-A)^2=-A \Rightarrow A=[0] \)

Theorem 6

Let A, B be idempotent matrices. A+B is idempotent iff AB=BA=0.

proof
\( (A+B)^2 = A^2+B^2+AB+BA=(A+B)+AB+BA. \\\ (A+B) \text{ is idempotent iff } AB+BA=0 \\\ AB+-BA \Rightarrow AABB=-ABAB \Rightarrow -AB=(AB)^2=(-AB)^2. \\\ BABA=-BBAA \Rightarrow -BA=(BA)^2=(-BA)^2 \\\ \text{ Thus, -AB and -BA are idempotent. } \)

\( (BA)^2=(-AB)^2=-AB=BA \text{. Since (AB), (BA), (-AB), (-BA) are idempotent. By Thm 5, AB=BA=0} \)
