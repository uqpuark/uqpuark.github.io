    \( \begin{aligned} { \partial \vec{a}^T\vec{\mu} \over \partial \vec{\mu} } &= { \partial \vec{\mu}^T\vec{a} \over \partial \vec{\mu} } = { \partial \over \partial \vec{\mu} }\sum_{i=1}^aa_iu_i \\\ &= [ { \partial \sum_{i=1}^aa_iu_i \over \partial \vec{\mu}_1 } \cdots { \partial \sum_{i=1}^aa_iu_i \over \partial \vec{\mu}_n } ] \\\ &= [ a_1, a_2, \cdots, a_n ] \\\ &= a^T \end{aligned} \)

  2. Let A be a nXn matrix \( a_{ij} \)
    \( \begin{aligned} { \partial \vec{\mu}^T A\vec{\mu} \over \partial \vec{\mu} } &= { \partial \over \partial \vec{\mu} }[\sum_{i=1}^au_ia_{i1} \cdots \sum_{i=1}^au_ia_{in}]\begin{bmatrix} \mu_1 \\\ \vdots \\\ \mu_n \end{bmatrix} \\\ &= { \partial \over \partial \vec{\mu} }\sum_{j=1}^n\sum_{i=1}^na_{ij}\mu_i \mu_j \\\ &= \begin{bmatrix} 2a_{11}\mu_1+\sum_{j=2}^na_{1j}\mu_j+\sum_{i=2}^na_{i1}\mu_i \\\ \vdots \\\ 2a_{kk}\mu_k+\sum_{j=1}^na_{kj}\mu_j+\sum_{j=1}^na_{ik}\mu_i \\\ 2a_{nn}\mu_n+\sum_{j=1}^{n-1}a_{nj}\mu_j+\sum_{j=1}^{n-1}a_{in}\mu_i \end{bmatrix}^T \\\ &=\begin{bmatrix} \sum_{j=1}^{n}a_{ij}\mu_j+\sum_{i=1}^{n}a_{i1}\mu_i \cdots \sum_{j=1}^{n}a_{nj}\mu_j+\sum_{i=1}^{n}a_{in}\mu_i \end{bmatrix} \\\ &= \begin{bmatrix} \sum_{j=1}^na_{1j}\mu_j+ \cdots \sum_{j=1}^na_{nj}\mu_j \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \sum_{i=1}^na_{i1}\mu_i+ \cdots \sum_{i=1}^{n}a_{in}\mu_i \end{bmatrix} \\\ &=\vec{\mu}^TA^T+\vec{\mu}^T A = \vec{\mu}^T (A+A^T) \end{aligned} \)

Mean, Variance, and Covariance of Quadratic Forms

(https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z0jBgMDkfUg)

Assume \( E(X)=\mu, Var(X)=\Sigma. \\\ Let A and B be nxn symmetric matrices. \)

① \( E(X^TAX) = tr( A\Sigma ) + \mu^TA\mu \)

proof
\( \begin{aligned} \Sigma&=E[(X-\mu)(X-\mu)^T] \\\ &= E[XX^T-X\mu^T-\mu X^T+\mu\mu^T] \\\ &= E[XX^T]-\mu\mu^T \\\ &\Longrightarrow E[XX^T]=\Sigma+\mu\mu^T \end{aligned} \)

\( \begin{aligned} E(X^TAX)&=tr(E(X^TAX))=E[tr(X^TAX)]=E[tr(AXX^T)] \\\ &=tr(AE(XX^T))=tr(A(\Sigma+\mu\mu^T))=tr(A\Sigma)+tr(A\mu\mu^T) \\\ &=tr(A\Sigma)+tr(\mu^T A\mu) \end{aligned} \)

tr(AB)=tr(BA), tr(A+B)=tr(A)+tr(B) quadratic form이 1x1 행렬이 되기때문에 tr을 붙여줘도 되고, E와 위치를 바꿔도 됨 1번째 줄에서 2번째 줄로 넘어가는 데 튕기면 직접 대입하면 확인 가능(참고)

* Will use "the Kronecker Product".

② \( Cov(X^TAX, X^TBX) \\\ = tr[(A \otimes B)E(XX^T \otimes XX^T)] - [tr(A\Sigma+\mu^TA\mu)][tr(B\Sigma)+\mu^T B\mu] \)

proof
\( \begin{aligned} E(X^TAXX^TBX)&=E[tr(X^TAXX^TBX)]=E[tr((X^TAX) \otimes (X^TBX))] \\\ & =E[tr((X^T \otimes X^T)(A \otimes B)(X \otimes X))]=E[tr((A \otimes B)(X \otimes X)(X^T \otimes X^T))] \\\ & =E[tr((A \otimes B)(XX^T \otimes XX^T))]=tr((A \otimes B)E(XX^T \otimes XX^T)) \\\ Cov(X^TAX, X^TBX) &= E(X^AXX^BX) - E(X^TAX)E(X^TBX) \end{aligned} \)

③ \( Var(X^TAX) = tr((A \otimes A)E(XX^T \otimes XX^T)) - [tr(A\Sigma)+\mu^TA\mu]^2 \)

proof
\( Var(X^TAX)=Cov(X^TAX, X^TAX) \)
The result follows from ②

Theorem 1
Let \( X \sim MVN_k(0, I) \) and \( A \) be a \((K \times K)\) Symmetric idempotent matrix with \( rank(A)=r \), then \( X^TAX \sim \chi_0^2(r) \)

proof
A 는 symmetric(대칭)이고, idempotent(멱등)이다. 따라서 decompose(분해) \( A=PDP^T \quad where P\)는 (KxK) orthogonal(직교)이고, \( D=diag(I_r, \vec{0}) \).
Theorem 3 in “Idempotent Matries"에 의해 Let \( Z=P^TX, then Z \sim N-K(0, I) since E(Z)=P^TE(X)-0 and Var(Z)=P^TVar(X)P=P^TIP=I. \)

using the first r components of Z, \( \sum_{j=1}^rZ_j^2 \sim \chi_0^2(r) \)

\( X^TAX = X^TPDP^TX=Z^TDZ=\sum_{j=1}^rZ_j^2 \sim \chi_0^2(r) \)

Theorem 2
Let \( X \sim MVN_K(0, \Sigma), \) where \( Sigma \) is positive definite(>). Let \( A \) be a \( (K \tiems K) \) symmetric matrix. If \( A\Sigma \) is idempotent and \( rank(A\Sigma)=r, then X^TAX \sim \chi_0^2(r). \)

proof

Theorem 3
Let A be a (nxn) matrix. If \( A^i=A^{i+1} \) for some i>0 then A is idempotent.

proof
Let \( \lambda_K\ K=1, \cdots, n \) be the eigenvalues(고유값) of A. Then \( \lambda_K^i \) are the eigenvalues of \( A^i \).
\( \begin{aligned} & \lambda_K^i v=A^i v=A^{i+1} v \Rightarrow \lambda_K^i =\lambda_K^{i+1} \quad K=1, \cdots, n. \\\ & \lambda_K={0 \text{ or } 1} \quad \forall K \Rightarrow \text{A is idempotent by “Idemtptoent Matrices”} \end{aligned} \)

Theorem 4
Let \( X \sim MVN_K(0, \Sigma) \text{ where } \Sigma \text{ is positive semi-definite.(>=)} \) Let A be a (KxK) symmetric matrix. \( \text{ If } \Sigma A\Sigma A\Sigma = \Sigma A \Sigma \text{ and } tr(A\Sigma)=r, \text{ then } X^TAX \sim \chi_0^2(r) \)

proof
use “The Spectral Decomposition”, “part1” Thm 2.
\( \Sigma=P \Lambda P^T \quad \text{where P is orthogonal and } \Lambda=diag(D, \vec{0} ) \quad \text{ where } D=diag(\lambda_1, \cdots, \lambda_K) \text{ where } l=rank(A). \)

\( A,\ P,\ \Lambda (K \times K), \ D (l \times l)\text{ with } \ l<K,\text{ D is non=signular} \)
