Hat Matrix

Hat Matrix

  • The Hat Matrix $$ H=X(X^TX)^{-1}X^T $$

    • Fitted model
      $$ \begin{aligned} &\hat{y}=X\hat{\beta} \\\ &=X(X^TX)^-1X^T\vec{y} \\\ &=H\vec{y} \end{aligned} $$
    • Residuals $$ \begin{aligned} &\vec{e}=\vec{y}-\hat{y} \\\ &=\vec{y}-H\vec{y} \\\ &=(I-H)\vec{y} \end{aligned} $$

  • Perpendicular Projection Matrix(PPM)

    • M is a PPM onto \( C(X) \), column space of X, if and only if
    1. \( \vec{V} \in C(X) \Rightarrow M\vec{V}=\vec{V} \)(projection)
    2. \( \vec{W} \in C(X)^\bot \Rightarrow M\vec{W}=\vec{0} \)(perpendicular)

  • H is a ppm onto \( C(X)^ \)

    1. Let \( \vec{V} \in C(X)\), then \( \vec{V}=X\vec{a} for \vec{a} \in \mathbb{R}^K+1 \)
      \( H\vec{V}= X(X^TX)^-1X^TX\vec{a}=X\vec{a}=\vec{V} \)
    2. Let \( \vec{W} \in C(X)^\bot \), then \( X^T\vec{W}=\vec{0} for \)
      \( H\vec{W}= X(X^TX)^-1X^T\vec{W}=\vec{0} \)

  • (I-H) is a PPM onto \( C(X)^\bot \)

    1. Let \( \vec{V} \in C(X)^\bot \), then \( H\vec{v}=\vec{0} \)
      \( (I-H)\vec{V}= \vec{V}-H\vec{V}=\vec{V} \)
    2. Let \( \vec{W} \in C(X) \), then \( H\vec{W}=\vec{W} for \)
      \( (I-H)\vec{W}= \vec{W}-H\vec{W}=\vec{0} \)

  • Geometric Illustration (동영상 참조)
    The response vector \( \vec{y} \) is decomposed into 2 orthogonal components. $$ \begin{aligned} &\vec{y}=\hat{y}+\vec{e} \\\ & \hat{y}=X\hat{\beta} \in C(X) \quad (Estimation Space) \\\ & \vec{e}=\vec{y}-\hat{y} \in C(X)^\bot (Error Space) \end{aligned} $$

  • Properties of H (H1) \( ( H=H^T \) ), (( HH^T=H \) idempotent
    (H2) \( (I-H)=(I-H)^T \), \( (I-H)(I-H)=(I-H) \)
    (H3) \( C(H)=C(X) \), perpendicular projection matrix
    (H4) \( C(I-H)=C(X)^\bot \)
    (H5) \( HX=X \)
    (H6) \( (I-H)X=0 \)
    (H7) \( Rank(H) = tr(H) = p+1 \)
    (H8) \( Rank(I-H) = tr(I-H) = n-(p+1) \)
    (H9) \( (I-H)H=0 \)
